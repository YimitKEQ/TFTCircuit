The Road to Pro

By Pro Player: KEQING/Levitate 20 min read

Becoming a professional Teamfight Tactics player is a dream for many, but few know the path to take. This guide outlines the steps you need to take to go from a casual player to a competitive pro. It's a long and difficult journey, but with the right mindset and work ethic, it's an achievable goal.

Step 1: The Grind

The first step on the road to pro is the grind. You need to play a lot of TFT to develop the muscle memory and game sense required to compete at the highest level. This means hundreds, if not thousands, of games. The goal is to reach the highest ranks of the ladder, as this is the primary way to get noticed by professional teams and tournament organizers.

Aim to play a consistent number of games each day and focus on improving one aspect of your gameplay at a time. Whether it's your economy management, your positioning, or your ability to pivot, there's always something to work on. Don't just play on autopilot; actively think about your decisions and their outcomes.

Step 2: Scrims & Networking

Once you've reached a high rank on the ladder, it's time to start playing in scrims. Scrims are practice matches against other high-level players, and they are an essential part of improving as a competitive player. The environment is more serious than a typical ladder game, and you'll be able to test your skills against the best of the best.

Networking with other players is also crucial. Join Discord servers, participate in online communities, and don't be afraid to reach out to other players for advice and practice. The TFT community is a tight-knit one, and the connections you make can open doors to opportunities down the road.

Step 3: Tournament Play

The final step is to start competing in tournaments. This is where you'll test your skills against the best players in the world and make a name for yourself in the competitive scene. Tournament play is a different beast than the ladder. The pressure is higher, and the competition is fiercer.

Start with smaller community tournaments and work your way up to larger events. Don't be discouraged if you don't see results immediately. Use each tournament as a learning experience. Analyze your gameplay, identify your weaknesses, and work on improving them for the next event.

Conclusion

The road to pro is long and challenging, but it's also incredibly rewarding. With dedication, hard work, and a little bit of luck, you can achieve your dream of becoming a professional TFT player. Stay focused, stay hungry, and never stop learning.
